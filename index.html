Narrative Text

Stories that entertain, inspire, and convey moral values

📌 Definition

What is Narrative Text?

Narrative text is a type of text that tells a story involving characters, setting, and a sequence of events. Its primary purpose is to entertain readers, though it often conveys deeper messages or moral values through the plot.

📚 Included Types

Examples of Narrative Text

Common story-based texts that entertain readers and deliver moral lessons.

Legends

Malin Kundang, Sangkuriang, Lake Toba, and Roro Jonggrang.

Fairytales

Cinderella, Snow White, and Beauty and the Beast.

Fables

The Lion and the Mouse or The Mouse Deer and the Crocodile.

Popular Fiction

Harry Potter, The Lion King, and other fictional stories.

Generic Structure

  • 01
    Orientation Introduces characters, setting (time and place), and the initial situation. It sets the scene for the reader.
  • 02
    Complication The main conflict or problem arises. This is the rising tension that drives the story forward and creates interest.
  • 03
    Resolution The conflict is resolved — either happily or unhappily. The characters find a way to deal with the problem.
  • 04
    Re-orientation (Optional) A closing section that reflects on the events, often delivering a moral or final insight about the story.
📖 Example Story

The Lion and The Mouse

Orientation

Once upon a time, a mighty Lion lay sleeping in the African savanna under the warm afternoon sun. His golden mane spread across the grass as he rested peacefully. Nearby, a tiny Mouse scurried through the meadow, looking for seeds to eat.

Complication

Without paying attention, the little Mouse accidentally ran across the Lion's nose. The Lion woke up with a roar, swiped his enormous paw, and caught the tiny creature. "How dare you disturb my sleep!" growled the Lion, lifting the Mouse close to his great jaws. "I shall eat you for this!"

The Mouse trembled with fear but managed to squeak boldly, "Please, great King, spare my life! I am too tiny to fill your belly. Let me go, and someday — I promise — I will repay your kindness." The Lion burst into laughter. A mouse repaying a lion? Amused by the little creature's boldness, he opened his paw and let the Mouse go free.

Resolution

Some weeks later, the Lion was prowling through the jungle when he stepped into a hunter's rope trap. The more he struggled, the tighter the ropes bound him, and his mighty roars echoed across the land. The little Mouse heard the familiar voice and rushed to the scene. Without hesitation, the Mouse began gnawing at the thick ropes with her sharp teeth. One by one, the strands snapped — and the Lion was free.

Re-orientation

"You laughed when I said I would repay you," the Mouse said with a smile. "Now you see: no act of kindness is ever too small, and even the mightiest may need the help of the tiniest." From that day on, the Lion and the Mouse became the best of friends — a reminder that kindness is never wasted, and true friendship knows no size.

Biography Text

The story of a real person's life, achievements, and legacy

📌 Definition

What is Biography Text?

A biography is a detailed written account of another person's life. It documents their life journey — from birth to significant life events — written by someone other than the subject, aiming to inform and inspire readers.

🏅 Included Types

Examples of Biography Text

Biographies of important figures from Indonesia and around the world.

National Figures

Ir. Soekarno, Ki Hajar Dewantara, and B.J. Habibie.

International Figures

Albert Einstein, Nelson Mandela, and Marie Curie.

Generic Structure

  • 01
    Orientation Introduces the subject — who they are, when and where they were born, and background information about their early life.
  • 02
    Events / Record of Events A chronological account of the significant events, achievements, challenges, and milestones in the subject's life.
  • 03
    Re-orientation A concluding section that reflects on the subject's legacy, lasting impact, or a summary of their contributions to the world.
🏅 Example Biography

Biography of B.J. Habibie

Orientation

Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie was born on June 25, 1936, in Parepare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia. Known as one of the most brilliant minds Indonesia has ever produced, he displayed exceptional intellectual talent from a very young age. Raised in a family that valued education deeply, young Habibie showed a remarkable passion for mathematics and engineering that would define his extraordinary life.

Events

After completing his early education in Indonesia, Habibie pursued aeronautical engineering at RWTH Aachen University in Germany in 1955. He graduated with outstanding grades and went on to earn his doctorate in aerospace engineering. His brilliance did not go unnoticed — he joined Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm (MBB), a leading German aircraft company, where he rose to become Vice President and pioneered the revolutionary "Habibie Factor," a mathematical formula for calculating the stress and fractures in aircraft structures.

In 1974, at the request of President Suharto, Habibie returned to Indonesia to lead the country's technological development. He founded PT Industri Pesawat Terbang Nusantara (IPTN) and produced the N250 Gatotkaca — the first passenger aircraft designed and built in Southeast Asia. He served as Minister of Research and Technology for over two decades and was elected Vice President in 1998. When Suharto resigned in May 1998 amid political turmoil, Habibie was sworn in as the 3rd President of Indonesia — a role he held until 1999.

As President, Habibie introduced sweeping democratic reforms: granting freedom of the press, releasing political prisoners, and allowing the first free elections in decades. He also made the difficult decision to allow a referendum in East Timor, which led to its independence — a move that was widely debated but reflected his commitment to democratic principles.

Re-orientation

B.J. Habibie passed away on September 11, 2019, but his legacy lives on as a symbol of intelligence, integrity, and dedication to Indonesia. He proved that an Indonesian could stand at the forefront of global science and technology. His life story continues to inspire millions of young Indonesians to dream big, study hard, and serve their nation with passion and excellence.

Recount Text

Retelling past events and personal experiences in sequence

📌 Definition

What is Recount Text?

A recount text is a piece of writing that retells past events or experiences in chronological order. Its main purpose is to inform or entertain the reader by sharing what happened during a specific time.

✏️ Included Types

Examples of Recount Text

Texts that retell personal experiences or historical events.

Personal Experiences

A holiday to the beach, first day at school, or winning a competition.

Travelogue

Camping with family or mountain hiking experiences.

Historical Recount

The Indonesian Independence Proclamation or the sinking of the Titanic.

Generic Structure

  • 01
    Orientation Sets the scene by answering who, what, where, and when — giving readers background context about the event.
  • 02
    Events A series of events told in chronological order. Each event is described in sequence, showing what happened step by step.
  • 03
    Re-orientation A closing comment, personal reflection, or summary of the writer's thoughts and feelings about the experience.
✏️ Example Recount

My Holiday to the Beach

Orientation

Last month, during the school holiday, my family and I decided to spend a long weekend at Parangtritis Beach in Yogyakarta. We had been planning the trip for weeks, and we were all so excited. We woke up very early on Saturday morning, packed our bags, loaded the car, and hit the road before sunrise.

Events

We arrived at the beach around 8 o'clock in the morning, just as the sun was rising over the horizon. The sight was breathtaking — the sky was painted in shades of orange and pink, and the waves crashed dramatically against the dark volcanic sand. The cool morning breeze was refreshing after the long drive.

First, my younger brother and I ran straight to the edge of the water, laughing as the waves chased our feet. Then, my father rented two horses so we could ride along the shoreline — it was my first time riding a horse, and it felt thrilling and a little wobbly! My mother, who preferred to stay on solid ground, set up a mat nearby and prepared our breakfast picnic.

After breakfast, we explored the beach further and discovered a traditional craft market where local vendors sold beautiful seashell souvenirs, batik shirts, and fresh coconut drinks. I bought a small seashell wind chime as a memento. In the afternoon, we watched a group of local fishermen haul in their nets — it was fascinating to see how hard they worked under the hot sun.

As evening approached, we gathered to watch the sunset together. The golden light reflecting on the waves was absolutely stunning. We took dozens of photos before reluctantly packing up and heading back to our hotel, tired but happy.

Re-orientation

That weekend at Parangtritis Beach was truly one of the most memorable family holidays we have ever had. The natural beauty of the beach, the joy of being together, and all the new experiences we shared made it a trip I will never forget. I hope we can go back again someday — and next time, I'll make sure to wake up early enough to see the sunrise from the very beginning.
